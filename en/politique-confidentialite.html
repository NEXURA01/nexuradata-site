Personal information protection

Privacy policy

This policy explains how NEXURA DATA collects, uses, shares, retains and protects personal information provided through the website, a service request or a communication related to a case.

Effective date: April 14, 2026
Last updated: April 25, 2026
Person responsible for the protection of personal information: Giacomo Navarro
Contact email: privacy@nexuradata.ca

~/summary In plain English

  • What we collect: what you write to us (name, email, device, description). Nothing else.
  • Why: to open your case, reply to you, do the work, invoice you.
  • Who sees it: our lab. Our technical providers (hosting, email, payment) only when strictly necessary.
  • How long: as long as your case is active, plus the legal retention period. Then secure destruction.

The full text below prevails in case of any inconsistency with this summary.

1. Scope

This policy applies to personal information collected by NEXURA DATA through its website, forms, emails, operational communications and, when a case is opened or accepted, in the course of managing, tracking, invoicing or processing payment for an intervention.

2. Personal information collected

Depending on the nature of the exchange, NEXURA DATA may collect only reasonably necessary information, including:

  • full name;
  • email address;
  • phone number;
  • type of device or media involved;
  • urgency level;
  • description of the issue or need;
  • exchanges related to the follow-up of a request or case;
  • case identifiers and access mechanisms generated for tracking purposes;
  • administrative, contractual or payment information needed when an intervention is authorized.

3. Purposes of collection and use

Personal information is used to:

  • receive and qualify a request;
  • open, manage and track a case;
  • communicate with the person concerned regarding an assessment, quote, authorization, payment, update or next step;
  • protect the security, integrity and traceability of operations;
  • maintain administrative, technical, accounting and service records;
  • comply with legal, tax, contractual, security, fraud prevention and dispute resolution obligations.

4. Consent and communications

When personal information is submitted through the website form or by email, it is used to process the request, communicate about the case and ensure operational follow-up of the service.

This consent does not, on its own, constitute consent to receive promotional or marketing communications. Any separate marketing communication must rely on separate consent where required by law.

5. Service providers and disclosure to third parties

NEXURA DATA relies on a limited number of subprocessors to operate the website, host its operational data, send transactional communications, process payments and serve certain technical resources (for example web fonts). At the time this policy was last updated, those subprocessors are:

  • Cloudflare, Inc. (United States) — site hosting, CDN, firewall, DNS and D1 database. Receives the visitor's IP address, HTTP headers and the contents of submitted forms (case form, quote request, newsletter sign-up, etc.).
  • Google LLC (United States) — delivery of Google Fonts loaded from fonts.googleapis.com and fonts.gstatic.com when the site is rendered (transmits the visitor's IP address). Google Search Console receives our sitemap submissions and lets NEXURA DATA review aggregated, anonymous search statistics. Where applicable, Google Business Profile hosts our public listing.
  • Stripe Payments Canada Ltd. — payment processing whenever a deposit or payment is made through the site. Receives only the information needed for the transaction (never the content of a client case).
  • Resend, Inc. (United States) — delivery of transactional email (submission confirmations, case follow-ups, operational notifications).
  • Meta Platforms, Inc. (United States) — only when the person chooses to communicate via WhatsApp Business; this channel is strictly optional and is never used to transmit case contents.

In accordance with Quebec's Law 25, NEXURA DATA conducts privacy assessments before any transfer of personal information outside Quebec and limits such disclosures to what is reasonably necessary for the service. Media submitted and files recovered in the context of a case are never transmitted to these subprocessors; they are processed on the laboratory's infrastructure in Montreal.

The list above is updated as needed. Individuals may request further detail about the information shared with any specific subprocessor by writing to privacy@nexuradata.ca.

6. Payments and related information

When a payment is offered or accepted in the context of a case, the information needed to administer the payment, confirm the transaction, maintain records and comply with accounting or tax obligations may be used and shared with providers involved in processing that payment.

NEXURA DATA processes only the information needed for this purpose and relies on tools suited to secure payment processing.

7. Retention, security and destruction

Personal information is retained only for the period reasonably necessary to manage the request, deliver the service, track the case, comply with legal obligations and resolve disputes, where applicable.

Reasonable administrative, technical and organizational measures are in place to protect the confidentiality of personal information based on its sensitivity, use, the media involved and the operating context.

When retention is no longer necessary, information is securely destroyed or anonymized, as appropriate.

8. Artificial intelligence tools

NEXURA DATA may use artificial intelligence tools that run locally, on the laboratory's own infrastructure, to assist with certain technical operations such as triage, sorting or preliminary identification of recovered files (for example distinguishing photographs, office documents or system files).

These uses comply with the following commitments:

  • no client data, no recovered file and no personal information is sent to any external or third-party-hosted artificial intelligence service;
  • the models used run offline on laboratory workstations, with no outbound connection to a third-party AI provider during processing;
  • results produced by these tools are treated as operational suggestions and remain reviewed by a technician; no decision producing legal effects or significantly affecting the person concerned is made solely on an automated basis;
  • the person concerned may, upon written request, ask that no artificial intelligence tool be used in the handling of their case; a strictly manual handling mode is then applied.

Assistance tools used by the team for administrative or development purposes (for example writing or programming aids) never receive data from a client case.

9. Access, correction and complaints

Any person concerned may, subject to applicable laws, request access to personal information held about them, request its correction or file a complaint regarding its handling.

To exercise these rights or submit a privacy-related request, please contact the person responsible for the protection of personal information at the coordinates indicated in this policy.

10. Privacy incidents

If a privacy incident affects personal information, NEXURA DATA assesses it promptly, takes reasonable measures to reduce the risks, documents the event and acts in accordance with applicable obligations, including notification where required.

11. Privacy governance

NEXURA DATA maintains privacy governance practices, including:

  • assignment of privacy responsibilities;
  • retention and destruction rules;
  • a process for handling privacy-related complaints and requests;
  • a framework for managing privacy incidents.

12. Additional information

Additional information about intervention conditions, payment terms, site commitments and general contact details may also be found in the Legal Notice, service pages, quote documents or communications sent as part of a case.
