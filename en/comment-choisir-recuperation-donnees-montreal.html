Buyer's guide

How to choose a data recovery service in Montreal

Honest guide written by a local lab to help you evaluate a firm before handing over your media. Criteria, red flags, realistic price ranges, and the questions to ask before signing.

1 · Verifiable physical address in Quebec

A real lab has a civic address listed at the Registraire des entreprises (NEQ). Several brands visible in Montreal are collection points that ship to Ontario or the United States — legal, but it changes the chain of custody, shipping delays, and your recourse if something goes wrong.

2 · Who physically handles your media

Ask explicitly: "Is the work done in-house or outsourced?" For sensitive cases (forensic, HR, legal, insurance), out-of-province subcontracting can complicate Law 25 compliance and evidence tracking.

3 · Written firm quote before any work

A professional diagnostic produces a firm quote with amount, deadline, and deliverables list — not an open range. Being asked to pay before the lab even knows the state of your media is a red flag.

4 · "No data, no charge" — in writing

The Canadian standard is clear: if recovery fails, you don't pay for the recovery work. But read the fine print — some fees (donor parts, cleanroom if already engaged, transport) may still apply. Everything must be in the quote before the mandate begins.

5 · Documented chain of custody

For any legal, insurance, HR, ransomware, or litigation case, demand a signed log: numbered seal at intake, SHA-256 hash of the disk image, timestamped log of every handling, operator + witness signature. Without this, your evidence may be challenged in court.

6 · Quebec Law 25 compliance

Since September 2023, anyone processing personal information in Quebec must have a designated Personal Information Protection Officer (PIPO), a published privacy policy, and documented subcontractors. Ask for these three by email — a compliant lab will reply within hours.

7 · Delivery and post-mandate erasure

The agreement must specify: delivery medium provided (external HDD, encrypted link), file format, retention period for internal copies (NEXURADATA: 30 days by default, adjustable), certified erasure procedure (DoD 5220.22-M or equivalent), and physical destruction of damaged media on request.

Realistic 2026 price ranges in Quebec

  • Simple logical case (deletion, formatting, lost partition): $350–$800
  • HDD with mechanical failure (heads, motor, PCB): $800–$1,800
  • SSD with controller failure: $900–$2,200
  • RAID 0/1/5/6 or multi-disk NAS: $1,200–$3,500
  • Phone (model, damage, encryption dependent): $600–$2,200
  • Apple Silicon Mac with soldered SSD: case-by-case, often unrecoverable
  • Forensic for litigation (report + testimony): from $2,500
  • Rush 24–48 h service: 50–100 % surcharge

Beware of bait prices under $200 and "flat fees" — serious recovery depends on the diagnostic.

Red flags

  • No civic address on the website, only a phone number.
  • Verbal quote over the phone with no media diagnostic.
  • Full payment requested before evaluation.
  • Refusal to disclose where the work will be done.
  • "100 % recovery guarantee" — no one can promise this.
  • No privacy policy or PIPO contact.
  • Google reviews with no dates or all posted in the same week.
  • Unrealistic deadline ("done in 2 hours") for a physical failure.

Questions to ask before signing

  1. Where exactly will the work be done? (city, country)
  2. Is the diagnostic free, and within what time frame?
  3. What is the firm quote before you touch the media?
  4. If recovery fails, what fees still apply?
  5. Can you provide a signed chain of custody?
  6. Do you have a designated Privacy Officer (Law 25)?
  7. How long do you retain my data after delivery?
  8. Do you accept traceable payments (Interac, e-transfer, card) in addition to cash?
