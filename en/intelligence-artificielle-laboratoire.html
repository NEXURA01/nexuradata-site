Artificial intelligence at the service of recovery

NEXURADATA integrates artificial intelligence models that run locally on the lab's own infrastructure to accelerate triage, sorting and identification of recovered files. No client data, no recovered file is sent to any external AI service. Human supervision remains at every step.

What AI does at the lab

  • Smart triage — automatic distinction between photos, office documents, system files, videos, archives. The client receives a delivery already organized by category.
  • Photo classification — grouping by subject, scene or person to quickly find priority memories within thousands of recovered images.
  • Document OCR — text recognition on scanned PDFs, invoices, contracts and notarized deeds to make documents searchable.
  • Deduplication — detection of duplicates and near-duplicates to reduce delivered volume and ease browsing.
  • Modified-extension detection — recognition of the actual format of a file whose extension was lost or altered by the incident.
  • Sensitive-file flagging — preliminary identification of documents likely to contain personal information (PII), so the client can validate them first.

Why local and not in the cloud

  • Privacy first — recovered files often contain personal, medical, financial or strategic information. They never leave the lab.
  • Quebec Law 25 compliance — no transfer to a foreign third party, no privacy impact assessment to redo for every case.
  • Operational independence — triage continues to work even during a network outage, a cloud provider failure, or a consumer AI service blocking.
  • Reproducibility — the same model, the same version, the same parameters yield the same result on the same file. Critical for expert-witness work.

What AI does NOT do (and why)

  • No automated decisions — AI suggests, a technician validates. No deliverable is released without human review.
  • No content analysis without mandate — models classify by type and structure, not by opinion on content.
  • No file rewriting — AI works on copies. Original artifacts and forensic images remain intact.
  • No training on client data — the models are not retrained on files from any case.
  • Opt-out available — the client may, in writing, request strictly manual handling with no AI tooling. See section 8 of the privacy policy.

Concrete client benefits

  • Organized delivery — instead of receiving 80,000 files in bulk, you receive a clear tree: photos / documents / videos / system, with sub-classification when useful.
  • Reduced turnaround on large cases — automated triage saves hours of manual work on multi-terabyte drives.
  • Searchable index — on request, delivery includes a keyword index for the OCR'd documents.
  • Transparent prioritization — you validate the categories that matter to you first (family photos, contracts, medical files) before the rest.

Ethical framework and compliance

  • Quebec Law 25 — declared use in privacy policy, section 8. Right to opt out guaranteed.
  • No solely-automated decisions — compliant with section 12.1 of Quebec's Act respecting the protection of personal information in the private sector.
  • Operations log — every run is recorded in the case session log (model used, version, parameters, responsible technician).
  • Secure destruction — working images and generated indexes are destroyed with the rest of the case file after the agreed retention period.
  • No AI on communications — your emails, calls and messages to the lab are handled by humans.

For enterprise mandates

For law firms, insurers, internal IT teams and compliance officers, NEXURADATA can provide on request:

  • a technical fact sheet on the AI environment in use (models, versions, local execution, network isolation);
  • a written attestation confirming that no file from the case was transmitted to a third-party AI service;
  • the operations-log extract corresponding to the mandate;
  • a strictly manual handling mode on request (no AI assistance whatsoever).
