How it works

Data recovery process in Montreal

A serious process should be easy to understand. The goal is to quickly qualify the case, protect the device, estimate the treatment path and return readable data within the agreed scope.

1. First contact

The starting point is to describe the device, observed symptoms, actions already attempted and urgency level. This step helps avoid wrong direction from the start.

Sensitive information and devices should not be sent without prior coordination. The channel and delivery method must be confirmed before transmission.

2. Drop-off, pickup or shipping

Depending on the case, the device can be brought to the lab, entrusted via secure transport or handled according to the case requirements.

When shipping is planned within the accepted framework, NEXURADATA can arrange delivery via Canada Post according to the terms set at the quote stage.

3. Triage and technical assessment

The device is identified, qualified and examined to distinguish a simple deletion, a logical failure, a physical failure, a sensitive RAID environment or a more formal preservation need.

4. Chain of custody and write-blocked imaging

Before any handling, the media is logged, photographed and sealed. A bit-for-bit image is captured through a hardware write-blocker (Tableau, Atola), with a signed cryptographic hash (SHA-256). The original media is no longer touched — all subsequent work runs on the image.

5. Direction and quote

Once the initial assessment is complete, the treatment path is clarified with urgency level, known risks, case limitations and the chosen intervention framework. No billable work starts without written approval.

6. Recovery or specialized treatment

Logical recovery on the image, cleanroom intervention for mechanical HDDs, NAND chip-off read for SSDs / mobile, offline RAID reconstruction. The method is logged in the technical file — useful for insurance, audit, litigation.

7. Verification and integrity

Recovered data is hash-validated, organized to mirror the original tree where possible, and counted: file count, recovered volume, success rate, known limits. You receive a summary before delivery.

8. Encrypted return and destruction

Delivery on a new AES-256 encrypted media or secure transfer. On request, certified destruction of working copies after the agreed retention period. Law 25 compliance.

When the method changes

  • A clicking drive is not treated like a simple file deletion.
  • A RAID should not be handled like a standard external drive.
  • A sensitive internal case may require more than a simple recovery.
  • An available backup does not always replace analysis of the source device.
